UEFA Player of the Year nominees list

Lionel Messi

It’s hard find the words to describe a man that the football world known simply as ‘The GOAT’. Born in Rosario, the Argentine crossed the Atlantic at just 13 years of age to join FC Barcelona. 21 years later, the famous left foot has dribbled its way to Paris Saint-Germain, for the biggest challenge of his career. In between, ‘Leo’ has scaled all the heights. Messi has won 4 Champions League, 10 Liga titles, 7 Copa del Rey, 6 European Golden Boots, an Olympic gold medal and lifted the Copa America with Argentina, just before landing in the French capital.

Kylian Mbappe

Mbappé began his senior career with Ligue 1 club Monaco, making his professional debut in 2015, aged 16. With them, he won a Ligue 1 title, Ligue 1 Young Player of the Year, and the Golden Boy award. In 2017, he signed for league rivals Paris Saint-Germain on an initial loan, which was made permanent in 2018 in a transfer worth €180 million plus add .

C.Ronaldo

On 15 June 2021, Ronaldo scored twice in Portugal's first game of Euro 2020, a 3–0 win against Hungary in Budapest.[368] This took him to a total of eleven European Championship goals, two clear of Michel Platini, as the all-time top goalscorer in the competition's history.[369] He also became the first player to score at five European Championships,[370] and in eleven consecutive tournaments. The brace made Ronaldo the oldest player to score two goals in a match in the European Championship,.

Neymar Jr.

On 13 June 2021, in Brazil's opening group match of the 2021 Copa América, which took place on home soil, Neymar scored his team's second goal from the penalty spot and later assisted another for Gabriel Barbosa in a 3–0 win over Venezuela.[253] In the following match on 17 June, he once again scored the second goal in an eventual 4–0 win over Peru.[254] On 23 June he assisted Casemiro's match–winning goal from a corner in injury time of a 2–1 win over Colombia.

Lewandowski

On 28 March, he scored two goals in a 3-0 home win against Andorra in a World Cup qualification match, and also damaged ligaments in his right knee (he was taken off after 63 minutes); hence, he missed both Champions League quarter-final matches against Paris Saint-Germain, in which Bayern Munich lost on away goals rule after a 3–3 draw on aggregate.[168] On 24 April, he returned after almost a month on the sidelines in a 1–2 defeat to Mainz, where he scored in added time.

Rudiger

Rüdiger was included in Germany's final 23-man squad for the 2018 FIFA World Cup on 4 June 2018.[36] On 23 June, he was picked up for the second group stage match against Sweden as first choice centre-back Mats Hummels suffered an injury, the game ended 2–1 win for Germany.[37][38] Four days later, he did not play the last group stage match and his side were knocked out by South Korea after a 2–0 defeat.[39] On 19 May 2021, he was selected to the squad for the UEFA Euro 2020.[

jorjinho

In June 2021, he was included in Italy's squad for UEFA Euro 2020 by manager Roberto Mancini.[54] On 6 July, following a 1–1 draw after extra-time against Spain in the semi-final of the competition, Jorginho scored the decisive penalty kick in the resulting shoot-out to send Italy to the final;[55][56] during the match, he completed a tournament high of eight interceptions.[57] In the final on 11 July against England at Wembley Stadium, after a 1–1 draw following extra time, his spot-kick was saved in the penalty shootout by Jordan Pickford, but Italy would go on to win 3–2 in the shootout.

N.Golo Kante

After Kanté's match-winning performances in Chelsea's run up to the final of the 2020–21 UEFA Champions League season, several pundits proclaimed him as the apparent successor to former France and Chelsea player Claude Makélélé, who is considered to be one of the best defensive midfielders of all time.

Erling Haaland

Haaland ended the season with 41 goals in all competitions, including 27 in the league, which won him the fan-voted Bundesliga Player of the Season award.[72] He also finished the season as the top scorer of the Champions League with ten goals.

DI Maria

Di María made his senior international debut for Argentina in 2008 at age 20, and has since earned over 100 caps, including appearances in eight major tournaments. He scored the goal that won the country gold at the 2008 Olympics, and featured in the side that reached the final of the 2014 FIFA World Cup, the 2015 Copa América and the Copa América Centenario, and won the 2021 Copa América.

Ederson

Ederson made his senior debut for Brazil in 2017 after previously being capped at under-23 level. He was chosen in Brazil's squad for the 2018 World Cup and both the 2019 and 2021 Copa América, winning the 2019 tournament.

Sergio Ramos

Ramos currently holds the record as the most capped player in the history of the Spanish national team, and is his country's eighth-highest all-time goalscorer. He also holds the record of most international victories for Spain with 131 matches won.
